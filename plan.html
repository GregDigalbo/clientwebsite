Strategic Plan (2015-2018)

  1. Identify Hispanic/Latino faculty, staff, graduate students, and allies at Virginia Tech and encourage their participation in the Caucus. The purpose here is to build our ranks and ensure wider representation.

  2. To increase Caucus participation in undergraduate student affairs. Examples include working closely with LASO and the TechDREAMers, and the creation of undergraduate research symposiums and opportunities.

  3. Mentoring: Create tangible networks of mentoring among different members of the Caucus. Create a system of assigned mentors for all new members to the community. Mentoring should also extend to the Latino graduate student community.

  4. Recruitment: Identify current statistics regarding constituents in the different roles (faculty, staff, students) in the institution. Work to increase these numbers through partnering with relevant organizations and offices on campus.

  5. Retention: Ensure that VT provides a competitive package and benefits for all Caucus members. Ensure that issues of salary equity for current members is at the forefront of any retention efforts made by the university.

  6. Space: Work towards the creation of a dedicated Center for Hispanic/Latino Affairs, which has been part of the InclusiveVT conversation.

  7. Endowed Chair: Work towards creating an endowed chair in Hispanic/Latino Studies.

  8. Academic: Work towards the implementation and development of a Latino American Studies program, which starts with a Pathways Minor.

  9. Education: Give presentations to Academic Advisors, the Presidential Inclusion and Diversity Executive Council, and Diversity Development Institute about Hispanic/Latino culture and identity.

  10. Latina/o Intercultural Engagement Center Faculty Fellow: Work with the Division of Student Affairs and the Provost towards the establishment of a faculty fellow or post doctoral fellowship in IEC.

  11. State Representation: Participate in statewide dialogue about Hispanic/Latino issues.
